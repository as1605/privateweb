How to create a user web page like this

  1. Connect into IITD VPN if you're not on campus. Make sure to latest version of OpenVPN Connect client
  2. Connect to your CSC machine using SSH. Go to your terminal (bash/zsh/command prompt) and enter command in format ssh ee1200461@ssh1.iitd.ac.in where you replace ee1200461 with your own kerberos id. Accept all certificates, and then type in your password and press enter.
  3. Create a private_html folder in your home (~) directory 
    cd ~
mkdir private_html
cd private_html
  4. Create a file index.html in that folder, this, by default will be your home page when someone hits your website. You can explore more details here. 
    touch index.html
vim index.html
i
<html>
	<head>
		<title>My Home Page</title>
	</head>
	<body>
		Hello World!
	</body>
</html>
:wq
  5. Congrats! You have created your user web page! This will be accessible inside the campus network as well as through IITD VPN. You can learn further about HTML or PHP to see what all you can do! Do share your ideas and your webpage with me :)

A little note from my side

5 April 2021

1,03,794. We had 1,03,794 new cases of covid yesterday. And we can do nothing about it. The curve is steep, and it might take at least 3-4 months for it to fall again. If the institute decides to extend the online semester, we cannot change their decision, and we should not.

All we can do is change how we cope with it and how we can make it easier for ourselves.

Posts and memes and rants and requests about how much we want to go to campus are almost as old as the lockdown itself, but they will only frustrate us more and increase our stress after one point. Yeah, we get it, we want to go to the campus, it is one of the best places for us to be at, and much more than that, the idea of getting back to campus feels close to one of the things getting back to normal, of undoing all the damage that we went through last year.

But that should not mean that we have to think our lives online should be miserable or that we have to be sad or frustrated about it until we finally get to be on the campus.

At least we can improve our quality of life online and stay connected, even if you have been an introvert otherwise. Try to keep things very friendly and try not to do anything which would bother people. Also, avoid sharing things that would just add to the negativity. We can at least create a positive atmosphere for ourselves and a strong support system. Also, a little venting is okay, but don't let it go to the point where it just brings down the morale, that there is no hope in the subject and the course itself is bad, or that the online semester is a complete waste of our college life.

Even if the professors don't help, we still can work and help each other out in studying. Ask around in the groups, attend the extra sessions by mentors, explore the topics on the internet, and keep sharing anything of help you find.

At the end of the day, you need not stress yourself out too much over academics. Just do whatever you can do, staying in your comfortable range, and have some personal time to take of yourself. We have had a competitive nature, but you do not necessarily need to stretch yourself out for everything. It is more important to keep your spirit and interest alive rather than burning out. It is okay if you get a grade less, it is a price worth paying for keeping your health well. That lecture is not worth pulling an all-nighter for.

Don't stretch yourself out too much if you're unable to complete the syllabus. (Maybe just go through the tut solutions and attempt whatever you know in the exams.) Don't let bad results of any test cripple you from moving forward, and do not let it make you feel like a loser if it went bad (or a winner if it went great). Just say lol and move on. There is not enough time to feel bad about it. You have to walk it off and focus on what is next.
